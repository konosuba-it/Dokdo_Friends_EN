Resources like coal and oil have been heard a lot. So, have you heard about methane hydrates?
We're sure most of you don't know. Let's find out.

Methane Hydrate, nicknamed the burning ice

Methane Hydrate's nickname is Burning Ice. It looks like ice, but it's a nickname that you get from being able to light it.

Under high atmospheric pressure, even if the water falls below 0℃(32℉), it does not become ice and becomes between water and ice.
When natural gas enters it, it becomes a gas hydrate.

Huh? You said methane hydrate before.
But why is it gas hydrates instead of methane hydrates? You can ask. Methane hydrate is called because most of the natural gas that goes through the ice is methane gas.

Methane hydrate buried in Dokdo

Dokdo has a large amount of methane hydrate.
For the first time in 2005, 'Gas Hydrate Development Project' was launched and it was confirmed that methane hydrate was buried near Dokdo in 2007.

The reserves of methane hydrate, which is buried near Dokdo, are estimated to be about 600 million tons(≒£ 1.32 billion).
Some analysts say this is why Japan, which has methane hydrate extraction technology, is Dokdo is their territory insisting.

Advantages of Methane hydrate

Methane hydrate is mainly methane gas.
So it emits less pollutants than petroleum. So we are attracting attention as eco-friendly energy.

The combustion of methane produces only water and carbon dioxide, and the amount of carbon dioxide generated is much less than other fossil fuels.
That's why methane hydrate is a resource of the future.

Technical limit

Methane hydrate is environmentally friendly energy.
However, when released into the air during the drilling process, it causes a greenhouse effect 10 times more severe than carbon dioxide.

More technology development is needed to have the technology to eliminate methane emissions from the drilling process.
